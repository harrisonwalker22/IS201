Ever Since a young age, I would find cleaver and tricky ways to get what I wanted. It seemed to be a negative trait I've had, but over the years, I've learned to make it one of my strengths.

In my hometown growing up, there was a store that alwasy displayed "Free Cookies." Being a kid and loving cookies, I would take the chance to get a cookie everytime we went to the store. Sometimes, I would even pester my mother to stop at the store to grab a quick cookie. One day, while running errands with my mom (keep in mind I'm only 3 years old), we were about to pass the store that displayed the free cookies. Of course I had to say something to my mom, I didn't want to let those cookies be too lonely. I persistantly begged my mom to stop at the store so I could get a cookie. felt as though I desearved that cookie. I mean, I have been running errands with my mom. Doesn't that count for something? Whatever I did to try to convince my mom to stop failed. She said that we had other errands to do before picking up my brothers from school. After being rejected many times, I sat in my carseat pondering. "How could I still get that cookie even when Mom says we can't stop?" After about a minute or so I came up with a solution. With extreme confidence, I said, "Mom. Just let me drive."

This experience taught me, and my mom, a lot. It taught me that me, as Invictus states, "I am the master of my fate." If I want something, I can get it. It will sometimes take creative and unique ways to accomplish my end goal, but I am willing to do whatever it takes. The best thing about this process is that am learning all along the way.
